What's in our beer?

Brewing barley in Canada is about 20% Of all the barley produced here and is considered a speciality crop. Other grains that can be used include wheat, rye, oats and sorghum. Each of them has advantages, but barley continues to be the majority of all grains used in the brewery. Even in wheat beer, the proportion of barley is generally higher than 50%. This is due to the reality that other grains are malted without pods and are difficult to filter on their own. Malt provides a spectrum of beer colour that goes from pale straw to black. The pale malt creates the lighter colour and flavours observed in the easier beers to drink and soak thirst while the dark malts are responsible for richer and more robust colours in wearers and stouts.
